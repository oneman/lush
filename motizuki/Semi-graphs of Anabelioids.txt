[Mzk8]]. The original motivation of the present work lies in the problem of Ô¨Ånding
an appropriate and eÔ¨Écient way of representing, via categories, the geometry of

The main results of this paper may be summarized as follows:
(1) In ¬ß1, we study the geometry of semi-graphs, and in particular, expose
a proof related to the author by M. Matsumoto of a sort of analogue for
certain types of morphisms of Ô¨Ånite semi-graphs of ‚ÄúZariski‚Äôs main theorem‚Äù in scheme theory [cf. Theorem 1.2]. This result has some interesting
group-theoretic consequences related to the author by A. Tamagawa [cf.
Corollary 1.6]; in addition, it admits an interesting interpretation from a
more ‚Äúarithmetic‚Äù point of view [cf. Remark 1.5.1].
(2) In ¬ß2, we begin our study of the geometry of semi-graphs of anabeloids.
Our main result [cf. Corollary 2.7] concerns certain properties of the
commensurator in the proÔ¨Ånite fundamental group associated to a graph
of anabelioids of the various subgroups associated to subgraphs of the
given graph of anabelioids.
(3) In ¬ß3, we take up the study of ‚Äútempered fundamental groups‚Äù [i.e.,
roughly speaking, fundamental groups that correspond to coverings dominated by the composite of an arbitrary Ô¨Ånite covering and a [not necessarily Ô¨Ånite] covering of ‚Äúsome‚Äù associated semi-graph ‚Äî cf. [AndreÃÅ]], by
working with ‚Äútemperoids‚Äù, i.e., the analogue for tempered fundamental
groups of Galois categories [in the case of proÔ¨Ånite groups]. Our main result [cf. Theorem 3.7; Corollary 3.9] states that for certain kinds of graphs
of anabelioids, the vertices (respectively, edges) of the underlying graph
may be recovered from the associated tempered fundamental group as the
[conjugacy classes of] maximal compact subgroups (respectively, nontrivial intersections of distinct maximal compact subgroups) of this tempered
fundamental group. We then apply this result to show, in the case of
hyperbolic curves over p-adic local Ô¨Åelds, that the entire dual semi-graph
with compact structure may be recovered solely from the geometric tempered fundamental group of such a curve [cf. Corollary 3.11].
(4) Although the tempered fundamental group furnishes perhaps the most
eÔ¨Écient way of reconstructing a graph of anabelioids from a naturally associated category, in ¬ß4, we examine another natural approach to this problem, via categories of localizations. This approach is motivated partly by

In this Appendix, we discuss a certain minor generalization of the notion of a
